Study on Impact of COVID-19 on Consumer Finance

Duration: Jun 2021 - Mar 2022

Institution: Saintgits College of Applied Sciences

🔍 Project Overview 🔍

This study explores how the COVID-19 pandemic reshaped the consumer finance landscape and behavioral patterns of middle-class families in Kottayam District. It highlights significant shifts in financial behaviors, including a marked rise in digital payment adoption, a decrease in savings, and an increase in consumer debt due to economic stress. The findings provide valuable insights into how global crises can influence long-term consumer financial habits.

🌟 Project Highlights 🌟

  • Behavioral Analysis: Examined shifts in consumer behavior during the pandemic, focusing on digital payment systems and changes in spending habits.
  • Impact on Savings: Identified a significant decline in savings rates among middle-class families, attributed to financial stress and reduced income during the pandemic.
  • Increase in Debt: Explored the rise in consumer debt as a result of increased reliance on credit and loans during the crisis.
  • Policy Implications: The study also suggests potential policy changes that could support financial stability in future crises.

🛠 Tools and Technologies Used 🛠

  • IBM SPSS: Utilized for in-depth data analysis and statistical modeling to identify key trends in consumer behavior.
  • Microsoft Excel: Used for data cleaning, basic analysis, and generating insights from large datasets.
  • Microsoft PowerPoint: Created engaging presentations and visualizations to present the findings to stakeholders.
